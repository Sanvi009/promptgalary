Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is 'forced perspective' in photography?
    Forced perspective is a technique that uses optical illusion to make objects appear larger, smaller, farther, or closer than they actually are. Here, the sneakers in the foreground are made to look oversized compared to the subject.
  • Why use a 24mm lens for this shot?
    A 24mm lens is a wide-angle lens. It naturally exaggerates perspective, making objects closer to the camera appear much larger and creating a sense of depth, which is perfect for the forced perspective effect described.
  • What does 'all-blue monochrome studio' mean?
    It means everything in the studio environment—the walls, floor, chair, and even the car—is a shade of blue, creating a single-color, highly stylized backdrop that makes the subject and composition pop.
