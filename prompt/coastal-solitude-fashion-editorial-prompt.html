How to Use This Prompt

Start by uploading a clear reference photo of your face. Since this prompt demands exact facial and physical identity, your reference should be high quality with your features clearly visible. The final image will show you from a side profile at distance, so the AI needs to understand your bone structure, hair texture, and overall silhouette. Copy the complete prompt into your AI image generator. This prompt is built around a powerful concept: you as a small figure in a vast landscape, creating emotional resonance through scale and solitude. The ocean, the golden light, the wind—they're not just background; they're co-stars in this visual story. The setting is specific: 'dry dry grass facing the ocean' on a coastal landscape at golden hour. The repetition of 'dry' emphasizes texture and season—this isn't lush green grass but something more weathered, more poetic. You're 'sitting... hugging knees, body slightly curved'—a classic pose of introspection, protection, contemplation. The composition is an 'environmental portrait' where 'subject smaller within the frame, landscape visually dominant.' This isn't a close-up; it's a 'medium-long shot from further away' using a 'telephoto lens 100mm–135mm' for 'background compression.' The telephoto lens brings the distant ocean closer while keeping you at a respectful, observational distance. Despite the distance, 'facial expression still readable from side profile.' The AI must render your features with enough fidelity that viewers can sense your mood even from afar. 'Freckles, pores, natural skin imperfections visible even at distance, no beauty retouch' emphasizes editorial realism over idealized beauty. The mood is 'quiet introspective, solitude, poetic atmosphere, unposed candid moment.' You're not performing for the camera; you're simply existing in this moment, and the camera observes. This is 'observational photography style'—the photographer is present but invisible, capturing rather than directing. The lighting is 'side backlighting from sunset, soft rim light on hair.' The sun is low and to the side, creating that golden glow and highlighting your silhouette. 'Warm cinematic color grading' and 'film grain texture, analog film look' add nostalgic warmth and texture. Generate multiple versions to get the balance between figure and landscape, the wind in your hair, and that readable side profile exactly right.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why make the subject small within the frame rather than a close-up?
    Environmental portraits use scale to create emotional resonance. A small figure against a vast landscape emphasizes solitude, introspection, and the human place in the natural world. It tells a different story than a close-up—one about relationship to environment rather than just individual features.
  • What does 'background compression' from a telephoto lens achieve?
    Telephoto compression makes distant elements (like the ocean) appear closer to the subject, flattening perspective in a visually pleasing way. It keeps the subject and landscape connected in the frame while maintaining natural proportions and avoiding the distortion of wide-angle lenses.
  • How can facial expression be readable from a side profile at distance?
    The prompt specifies that expression should be 'still readable' through the silhouette and posture rather than detailed features. The curve of the body, the angle of the head, the way light hits the profile—these elements convey mood even when fine details aren't visible. It's about emotional communication through form.
