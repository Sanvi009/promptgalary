How to Use This Prompt

Begin by uploading a clear reference photo of your face to your AI image generator. A photo with good lighting and a neutral expression works well because the final image will add atmospheric effects. Copy the image link and paste it at the very beginning of your prompt field. Then add the complete prompt text. The key challenge here is balancing the sharpness of your face with the motion blur around the edges. The prompt asks for your face to be 'partially sharp' while the surroundings blur—this is a specific effect that may take a few tries to perfect. When you run the first generation, look for images where your features are recognizable but the edges have that ethereal, painterly softness. Pay attention to the neon light trails behind you—they should be red, orange, and white, creating those long exposure streaks. If the colors are wrong, you can specify more strongly: 'vibrant red and orange neon light trails.' If your face is too blurry, adjust to 'face clearly sharp, edges softly blurred.' The --ar 9:16 creates a vertical frame perfect for the city skyline backdrop. Run multiple generations, selecting the images that best capture that mysterious, otherworldly mood while keeping your identity intact. You might also experiment with different city skyline descriptions—'downtown metropolis' or 'waterfront city lights'—to vary the background while maintaining the core effect.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is 'long exposure' and how does the AI understand it?
    Long exposure is a photography technique where the shutter stays open longer, capturing movement over time—moving lights become trails, water becomes smooth, and motion blurs. The AI understands this concept from millions of long exposure photos in its training data, so it knows to render static elements differently from moving ones.
  • How do I keep my face recognizable with motion blur effects?
    The prompt specifies 'partially sharp' to balance identity with artistic effect. If your face isn't sharp enough, try 'face clearly sharp and detailed, only background and edges blurred.' If it's too sharp and loses the dreamy quality, use 'soft focus on face, ethereal glow.'
  • What does 'retro-futuristic film photography style' mean visually?
    It combines vintage film characteristics—grain, slight color shifts, softness—with futuristic elements like neon lights and modern cityscapes. Think 1980s sci-fi movie posters or Blade Runner aesthetics: warm glows, deep shadows, and a sense of tomorrow imagined from the past.
