How to Create an Ultra-Realistic Studio Portrait with Ultra-Wide Lens and Racing Jacket

AI Image Prompts
Published: 2026-02-27
Author: promptgalary

Generate a dramatic low-angle studio portrait using a 14mm ultra-wide lens, featuring a racing-style varsity jacket, hands reaching toward the camera, and professional cinematic lighting.

Generated Images

The Prompt 

Ultra-realistic studio portrait of me with exact same facial and physical identity from the uploaded image shot with a dramatic 14mm ultra-wide lens look, low-angle dynamic close-up with my hands reaching toward the camera to create immersive depth distortion. I'm wearing a green and black racing-style varsity jacket detailed patches over a crisp white shirt and patterned green tie, dark rectangular sunglasses, slightly wet textured hair . I'm looking slightly upward and sideways with a confident, focused expression arms raised and fingers partially framing the lens. Clean seamless mint-green studio backdrop with a smooth gradient, professional directional key light from above the side creating subtle jawline shadows, cinematic contrast, sharp natural skin texture, slight glossy highlights on the jacket, teal-green dominant editorial color grading, cool tone, high clarity, 8K full-frame, f/2.8, ISO 100, ultra-detailed RAW fashion photography, no illustration or cartoon.

How to Use This Prompt

Begin by selecting a high-quality reference photo of yourself and uploading it to your AI image generator. The reference should be well-lit with your face clearly visible, as the AI needs this to match your features accurately. Avoid photos where you're wearing sunglasses or have your hands in front of your face, since the final image will include these elements but the reference should be clean. Copy the entire prompt exactly as provided. Every detail, from the lens specification to the color grading, is carefully chosen to achieve a specific result. Paste it into your preferred AI tool like Midjourney, DALL-E 3, or Stable Diffusion. If your platform supports image prompts, upload your photo first and then add the text. Some tools work better with image URLs, so hosting your reference on a free site can be helpful. Generate multiple variations by using the re-roll or generate again feature. The ultra-wide lens effect with hands reaching toward the camera is a complex composition, so you may need several attempts to get the perspective exactly right. Look for results where the hand distortion looks intentional and artistic rather than awkward. After generating, examine the details closely. Check that the racing jacket patches are detailed and readable, the tie pattern is visible, and the sunglasses sit naturally on your face. If the wet hair texture isn't prominent enough, you can note that for a follow-up generation. Many platforms now offer inpainting tools to refine specific areas—use these to adjust any elements that aren't quite right while keeping the rest of the image intact.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why use a 14mm ultra-wide lens for a portrait?
    Ultra-wide lenses create dramatic perspective distortion that can make portraits more dynamic and immersive. When used creatively, they draw the viewer into the image and make the subject feel larger than life, which is perfect for fashion and editorial work.
  • How do I prevent my hands from looking too distorted?
    Some distortion is intentional with an ultra-wide lens, but if it's too extreme, try generating variations where your hands are slightly farther from the camera. The prompt specifies hands 'reaching toward' not 'touching' the lens, which helps balance the effect.
  • What does 'f/2.8' mean in photography terms?
    F/2.8 refers to the aperture setting of the lens. A wide aperture like f/2.8 creates a shallow depth of field, meaning the subject stays in sharp focus while the background blurs slightly. This helps the subject stand out from the backdrop.
