How to Use This Prompt

Start by uploading a clear reference photo of your face. This prompt demands exact facial and physical identity, so your reference should be high quality with your features clearly visible. A front-facing or three-quarter view works best for the AI to capture your unique bone structure and expression. Copy the complete prompt into your AI image generator. This prompt creates a specific fashion editorial aesthetic—vintage-inspired, richly colored, dramatically lit, with you as the sophisticated subject in a carefully styled environment. The setting is simple but luxurious: you're 'sitting on a velvet sofa' with a 'pastel green wall background.' Velvet suggests texture, richness, comfort. Pastel green provides a soft, complementary backdrop that won't compete with your outfit. The combination creates an intimate, studio-like atmosphere that focuses attention on you. The outfit is a deliberate color story: 'light blue blazer over a coral red top.' Blue and coral are complementary colors that create visual interest without clashing. The blazer adds structure and sophistication; the coral top adds warmth and vibrancy. You're 'holding a yellow smartphone'—a small pop of bright color that adds modernity to the vintage aesthetic. The lighting is 'soft dramatic lighting from the side' with a 'golden hour glow.' Side lighting creates depth through shadows, sculpting your features and adding mystery. Golden hour glow adds warmth and nostalgia, bathing the scene in that coveted late afternoon light. The color grading is specified as 'warm teal and orange'—a classic cinematic combination that creates visual tension and richness. Teal shadows, orange highlights—this palette has become synonymous with high-end film and editorial work. Technical specs: 'shallow depth of field' keeps focus on you while blurring the background. '85mm lens' is the classic portrait focal length, creating flattering compression. 'Soft film grain' adds texture and vintage feel. 'Ultra realistic, 4k' ensures sharp detail where it matters. Generate multiple versions to get the side lighting, color grading, and that perfect golden hour glow exactly right.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why teal and orange color grading specifically?
    Teal and orange is a classic cinematic color combination because the colors are complementary—they create visual tension and make skin tones (which fall in the orange range) pop against teal shadows. It's become synonymous with high-end film and editorial work.
  • What does 'soft dramatic lighting from the side' achieve?
    Side lighting creates depth by casting shadows across the face and body, sculpting features and adding mystery. 'Soft' ensures these shadows aren't harsh or unflattering—they're gentle gradients that model the form while maintaining a warm, inviting atmosphere.
  • Why include a yellow smartphone in a vintage aesthetic image?
    The yellow phone adds a pop of contrasting color and a contemporary element to the vintage scene. This tension between old and new creates visual interest and prevents the image from feeling like a costume drama—it's modern fashion with vintage soul.
